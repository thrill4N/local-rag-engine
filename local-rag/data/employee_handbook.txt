Employee Handbook — Time Off, Remote Work, and Expenses

Section 1: Paid Time Off
Full-time employees accrue 15 days of paid time off (PTO) per calendar year,
credited at a rate of 1.25 days per completed month of employment. Unused
PTO carries over into the following year up to a maximum of 5 days; any
balance beyond that is forfeited on December 31st. Employees must submit
PTO requests through the HR portal at least 5 business days in advance for
any absence longer than 2 consecutive days. Sick leave is tracked
separately from PTO and is not subject to the carryover cap.

Section 2: Remote Work Policy
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week without prior manager
approval. Fully remote arrangements (4-5 days per week) require written
approval from both the employee's direct manager and the department head,
renewed annually each January. Remote employees are expected to be
reachable during core hours of 10am-3pm in their local time zone,
regardless of which days they are working remotely.

Section 3: Expense Reimbursement
Business expenses under $75 do not require pre-approval and can be
submitted directly through the expense portal with a receipt attached.
Expenses of $75 or more require pre-approval from a manager before the
expense is incurred. All expense reports must be submitted within 30 days
of the expense date; reports submitted after 30 days will not be
reimbursed except in cases of documented extenuating circumstances approved
by Finance.

Section 4: Equipment and Technology Stipend
Employees working remotely at least 2 days per week are eligible for a
one-time $500 home office equipment stipend, and an ongoing $40/month
stipend toward internet costs. Stipend requests are submitted to HR and
require an itemized receipt for the one-time equipment portion.
